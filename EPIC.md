A good Epic should represent a large deliverable that can be broken down into multiple user stories. Below is the PRD organized into epics following an Agile/Scrum approach.

---

# Epic 1 — User & Identity Management

**Goal**

Allow users to securely create accounts, sign in, manage profiles, and personalize their experience.

### Features

* User registration
* Email verification
* Phone verification
* Login
* Social login (Google, Apple)
* Forgot password
* Logout
* Profile management
* Address management
* Food preferences
* Dietary restrictions
* Notification preferences

### Success Criteria

* User can register within 2 minutes.
* User can update profile information.
* User preferences are saved for AI recommendations.

## Suggested MVP Scope

To get BiteQuest to market quickly, focus on these epics first:

1. User & Identity Management
2. Restaurant Discovery
3. Menu & Ordering
4. Payments
5. Delivery Tracking
6. AI Food Concierge (core recommendations and "Surprise Me")
7. Restaurant Portal
8. Admin Portal
9. Notifications

The **Food Adventure (Gamification)**, **Social Food Community**, **Loyalty & Rewards**, and **Analytics & Insights** epics can follow in later releases to drive engagement and retention once the core ordering experience is stable. This phased approach reduces initial complexity while preserving the app's unique differentiators.
